About



Association football, more commonly known as football or soccer, is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players each, who primarily use their feet to propel a ball around a rectangular field called a pitch. The objective of the game is to score more goals than the opposing team by moving the ball beyond the goal line into a rectangular-framed goal defended by the opposing team. Traditionally, the game has been played over two 45-minute halves, for a total match time of 90 minutes. With an estimated 250 million players active in over 200 countries and territories, it is the world's most popular sport.

The game of association football is played in accordance with the Laws of the Game, a set of rules that has been in effect since 1863 and maintained by the IFAB since 1886. The game is played with a football that is 68–70 cm (27–28 in) in circumference. The two teams compete to get the ball into the other team's goal (between the posts and under the bar), thereby scoring a goal. When the ball is in play, the players mainly use their feet, but may use any other part of their body, except for their hands or arms, to control, strike, or pass the ball. Only the goalkeepers may use their hands and arms, and only then within the penalty area. The team that has scored more goals at the end of the game is the winner. Depending on the format of the competition, an equal number of goals scored may result in a draw being declared, or the game goes into extra time or a penalty shootout.

Internationally, association football is governed by FIFA. Under FIFA, there are six continental confederations: AFC, CAF, CONCACAF, CONMEBOL, OFC and UEFA. Of these confederations, CONMEBOL is the oldest one, being founded in 1916. National associations (e.g. The FA or JFA) are responsible for managing the game in their own countries both professionally and at an amateur level, and coordinating competitions in accordance with the Laws of the Game. The most senior and prestigious international competitions are the FIFA World Cup and the FIFA Women's World Cup. The men's World Cup is the most-viewed sporting event in the world, surpassing the Olympic Games.[6] The two most prestigious competitions in European club football are the UEFA Champions League and the UEFA Women's Champions League, which attract an extensive television audience throughout the world. Since 2009, the final of the men's tournament has been the most-watched annual sporting event in the world.

Top Players


Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi, the Argentine football virtuoso born on June 24, 1987, is an iconic figure in the world of soccer. Renowned for his extraordinary dribbling finesse, unparalleled playmaking vision, and prolific goal-scoring ability, Messi's name has become synonymous with excellence on the pitch. With a record-breaking tenure at FC Barcelona, where he amassed an astonishing collection of titles and accolades, including multiple Ballon d'Or awards, Messi's transition to Paris Saint-Germain in 2021 marked a significant shift in his illustrious career. Beyond his club achievements, his role in leading the Argentine national team to victory in the 2021 Copa America solidified his legacy as one of the sport's all-time greats, leaving an indelible impact on the beautiful game.


Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo, born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, stands as one of the most recognizable and accomplished figures in the history of soccer. Known for his incredible athleticism, exceptional goal-scoring prowess, and unwavering work ethic, Ronaldo has left an indelible mark on the sport. His journey has taken him through illustrious spells with Sporting CP, Manchester United, Real Madrid, Juventus, and a return to Manchester United in 2021. Throughout his career, he has consistently shattered records, claimed numerous Ballon d'Or awards, and displayed a remarkable ability to adapt his playing style as he aged, showcasing his versatility as both a winger and a forward. With his relentless drive for success, Ronaldo continues to inspire fans globally and remains a dominant force on the field.


Neymar

Neymar Jr., born on February 5, 1992, in Mogi das Cruzes, Brazil, is a dynamic and electrifying presence in the world of soccer. Renowned for his dazzling dribbling skills, flair, and creativity on the pitch, Neymar rose to prominence with Santos FC in Brazil before making a high-profile move to FC Barcelona in 2013. His partnership with Lionel Messi and Luis Suárez formed the iconic "MSN" trio that led Barcelona to numerous titles, including the UEFA Champions League. In 2017, Neymar transferred to Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) in a record-breaking deal. His time at PSG has been marked by impressive performances and a crucial role in the team's success. Neymar's contributions extend to the Brazilian national team, with whom he has showcased his talents in multiple Copa America and FIFA World Cup tournaments. Despite occasional controversies, Neymar's impact on the game's global stage is undeniable, making him one of the most recognizable and celebrated players of his generation.

Beautiful stadiums


Camp Nou, Barcelona

The Camp Nou stadium is the iconic home of FC Barcelona, one of the world's most renowned football clubs. Located in Barcelona, Spain, it's not only one of the largest football stadiums, but also a symbol of the club's rich history and passionate fan base.


Camp Nou, situated in the vibrant city of Barcelona, Spain, is one of the most illustrious football stadiums globally, boasting an unparalleled legacy and hosting the renowned FC Barcelona. With a seating capacity exceeding 99,000 spectators, it stands as Europe's largest football stadium, serving as a majestic arena where football magic unfolds. Camp Nou's hallowed grounds have witnessed the mesmerizing skills of legends like Lionel Messi, and its electrifying atmosphere during matches is a testament to the fierce loyalty of Barça's dedicated fans, collectively known as "culés." Beyond its remarkable role in football, Camp Nou stands as an architectural marvel, showcasing modernist design elements that echo Barcelona's artistic heritage. For football enthusiasts and tourists alike, a visit to Camp Nou offers an opportunity to immerse oneself in the spirit of the beautiful game and pay homage to a place that has become synonymous with football greatness.

Stepping into Camp Nou is akin to entering a sanctuary of football devotion. The stadium's history is etched into its very structure, from the echoes of the past that resonate through its corridors to the troves of trophies that have been celebrated on its turf. The Camp Nou Experience allows visitors to explore the club's rich history through its museum, offering glimpses into the club's storied past and showcasing memorabilia that immortalize unforgettable moments. The stadium's aura truly comes alive on matchdays when a sea of passionate fans don the club's iconic colors and chant anthems, creating an atmosphere that reverberates far beyond the field. Camp Nou isn't just a stadium; it's a living embodiment of the spirit of FC Barcelona, a place where dreams have been realized, and where the heart and soul of football continue to thrive.

Old Trafford, Manchester

Old Trafford stadium, located in Manchester, England, is the historic home of Manchester United Football Club, hosting countless memorable matches and moments, and standing as one of football's most iconic and revered arenaswhich was built in 1910.


Nestled in the heart of Manchester, England, Old Trafford stands as an iconic cathedral of football, encapsulating the rich history and heritage of Manchester United Football Club. With a seating capacity that exceeds 74,000, the stadium exudes an aura of football greatness, having witnessed the triumphs and trials of countless legends. From the "Busby Babes" to the "Class of '92," Old Trafford has been the stage for some of the most pivotal moments in football history. The Stretford End, known for its vocal and passionate support, adds to the stadium's allure, creating an electric atmosphere that encapsulates the very essence of football fandom. The "Theatre of Dreams," as it is affectionately known, has not only hosted memorable matches but has also become a pilgrimage site for fans around the world, eager to experience the hallowed grounds where dreams are made and memories are etched into football folklore.

Beyond its role as a football stadium, Old Trafford is a testament to Manchester United's enduring legacy. The Sir Alex Ferguson Stand pays homage to the club's legendary manager who led United to unprecedented success. The Museum & Tour, a treasure trove of history and memorabilia, offers visitors a chance to delve into the club's storied past, from its humble beginnings to its global prominence. The Munich Tunnel, a poignant reminder of the tragedy that befell the "Busby Babes," serves as a solemn tribute to those who lost their lives in the 1958 Munich air disaster. Whether it's the echoes of Sir Matt Busby's era or the modern-day exploits of players like Cristiano Ronaldo, Old Trafford stands as a living monument to the enduring spirit of Manchester United and the unwavering passion of its fans.
